# 🚕 Sweet Lift Taxi – Hourly Demand Forecasting

## The Business Problem

Sweet Lift Taxi needed to predict how many taxi orders would be requested in the next hour.

The goal was to improve driver allocation during peak hours and reduce passenger wait times.

---

## My Contribution

I built a forecasting model using historical airport taxi data to predict short-term demand.

The model was required to meet a business performance constraint:

> RMSE ≤ 48 (average prediction error under 48 orders).

---

## What I Did

- Cleaned and structured time-based historical data  
- Analyzed demand trends and peak-hour patterns  
- Engineered time-based features (previous demand, hour of day, day of week)  
- Trained and compared multiple machine learning models  
- Selected the model that balanced accuracy and reliability  

---

## Tools Used

Python | Pandas | Scikit-learn | Time Series Analysis

---

## Results

- Successfully developed a model that met the required accuracy threshold stablished by business.
- Identified clear seasonal demand patterns that support operational planning.
- Delivered a solution suitable for real-time demand forecasting.

(RMSE represents the average difference between predicted and actual taxi orders — lower is better.)
